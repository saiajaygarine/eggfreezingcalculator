Useful Information & Precautions

  • Consider the Risks: Egg freezing can be a safe and effective procedure, but it is not without risks. Some risks include ovarian hyperstimulation syndrome (OHSS), infection, and damage to the ovaries.
  • Age is Crucial: The younger you are when you freeze your eggs, the higher the success rates for later use. The quality of eggs tends to decrease with age, especially after 35.
  • Emotional and Financial Considerations: Egg freezing can be emotionally and financially challenging. It requires a strong commitment to your future goals, and the cost can vary depending on location and clinic.
  • Success Rates are Not Guaranteed: There is no guarantee that the frozen eggs will result in a successful pregnancy later. The success rate is influenced by factors such as egg quality, age, and health conditions.
  • Consult with Experts: Speak with a fertility specialist to fully understand the procedure, potential risks, and benefits. They can guide you in making an informed decision.

Eligibility Criteria for Egg Freezing

Who Can Take Egg Freezing?

  • Women who are in good overall health and have no significant ovarian or uterine problems.
  • Women under 35 years old (the younger you are, the better the quality of eggs).
  • Women who are not planning to have children in the near future but wish to preserve fertility.
  • Women with certain medical conditions that may affect fertility in the future (e.g., cancer treatments).

Who Should Take Egg Freezing?

  • Women who are not ready for children but want to preserve their fertility for later.
  • Women diagnosed with medical conditions that may reduce fertility, like cancer or endometriosis.
  • Women whose work or personal life might delay childbearing plans.
  • Women who have family members with a history of early menopause.

Who Should Avoid Egg Freezing?

  • Women over 40, as the success rate for egg freezing drops significantly after this age.
  • Women with poor ovarian reserve or a history of ovarian failure.
  • Women who do not want to undergo the hormone injections or other treatments associated with egg freezing.
  • Women with specific health concerns that could complicate the egg retrieval procedure (e.g., severe heart disease).
